## Market Size

**Population**
- WHO: 1.3 billion people experience significant disability globally — 16% of the world population, or 1 in 6.
- Return on Disability Group (*Global Economics of Disability: 2024*, broader definition): 1.6 billion people — 22% of the global population.
- 1 in 3 people will develop a disability in their lifetime.
- 75% of disabilities are non-apparent.

**Spending power**
- The disability market controls over $18 trillion in global spending.
- People with disabilities in North America and Europe alone control more than $2.6 trillion in disposable income.
- Source: Return on Disability Group, *The Global Economics of Disability: 2024*, via Benefits and Pensions Monitor: https://www.benefitsandpensionsmonitor.com/benefits/chronic-illness-disabilities/report-unveils-18-trillion-disability-market/388725

**Quote:** "The disability market is the largest emerging market in the world, yet materially all organizations fail to capture its full value." — Rich Donovan, CEO, Return on Disability Group

**WHO cost-benefit**
- WHO notes a $10 return for every $1 spent on implementing disability-inclusive prevention and care.
- Source: https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/disability-and-health

**SEO**
- Accessible websites rank for 27% more keywords and see higher organic search traffic — accessibility is also an SEO multiplier.
- Source: cited in G3ict/IAAP research — verify primary source before publishing.

## Financial Performance

Source: Accenture, *The Disability Inclusion Imperative* (2023), co-published with Disability:IN.
Based on 346 organizations tracked through the Disability Equality Index, 2015–2022.
This is an update to the 2018 *Getting to Equal: The Disability Inclusion Advantage* — the original source most existing citations reference.

- 1.6x+ revenue vs. competitors
- 2.6x+ net income vs. competitors
- 2x+ economic profit vs. competitors
- 25% more productive vs. competitors

**Employee disclosure data (same report)**
- 76% of employees do not fully disclose their disability at work.
- Only 4.6% self-identify their disability status with employers.
- Compounds the 75% non-apparent stat: most disabilities are invisible, and most employees with disabilities are actively not disclosing them — standard user research and internal employee data both systematically undercount the affected population.

Sources:
- Disability:IN portal: https://portal.disabilityin.org/
- Forbes coverage: https://www.forbes.com/sites/gusalexiou/2023/11/30/latest-accenture-research-confirms-disability-inclusive-companies-are-more-profitable/

## Legal Landscape

**United States — ADA**
- The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) applies to digital products.
- Web accessibility lawsuits have increased dramatically year over year — 2,200+ filed annually.
- *(Verify/update with current figure and source before publishing.)*

**European Union — EAA**
- The EU Accessibility Act (EAA) became enforceable June 28, 2025.
- Covers e-commerce, banking, smartphones, and computers — essentially any digital product or service sold in the EU.
- Mandates WCAG 2.1 Level AA compliance.
- Requires a public accessibility statement for each website and app.

**International standard**
- WCAG 2.2 was formally adopted as ISO/IEC 40500:2025.
- Elevates WCAG from an advisory guideline to an international standard now referenced in procurement contracts and legal defense.

**Implication for "we ran automated tools" defense**
- Automated tools have known, documented coverage gaps.
- "We ran automated tools" is not a defensible compliance posture.
- Only a genuine audit with human validation can establish meaningful compliance.

## Certifications & Accountability Frameworks

**Disability Equality Index (DEI)**
- A scored benchmark (0–100) from the American Association of People with Disabilities (AAPD) and Disability:IN.
- Companies scoring 80+ are recognized as "Best Place to Work for Disability Inclusion."
- The "Enterprise-Wide Access" category explicitly evaluates digital accessibility policies and WCAG 2.1 AA compliance.
- Public, named ranking — brand exposure cuts both ways.

**IAAP Business Accessibility Criteria (BAC)**
- From the International Association of Accessibility Professionals.
- Covers 14 topic areas: leadership, procurement, design/build/test, HR, communications.
- After six months of participation and data sharing, orgs receive a Business Digital Commitment Badge (via Credly) — a verifiable public signal that accessibility is embedded in strategy.

**ESG / CSRD**
- ESG frameworks now treat digital accessibility as an "ethical benchmark" in social performance reporting.
- The EU's Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) requires external assurance over ESG disclosures.
- WCAG 2.1/2.2 compliance is increasingly used as proof of social performance in ESG audits.
- Automated-tool results won't satisfy an ESG auditor. Documented, human-validated compliance will.

**B Corp**
- B Corp accreditation evaluates companies on social and environmental impact including how they treat all stakeholders.
- Accessibility is directly relevant to B Corp's equity and inclusion criteria.
- "Compliance theater" — passing automated tools without real validation — won't satisfy this. B Corp asks for substance.
- Note: B Corp certification is specifically for for-profit companies.

**ISO 26000 (Social Responsibility)**
- A guidance standard applicable to all organization types: private, public, and non-profit.
- Core subjects (Human Rights, Consumer Issues, Community Involvement) require digital accessibility as a tool for equity.
- Closest structural analog to B Corp for nonprofits and public sector orgs.

**Unifying point:** Every one of these frameworks requires substance, not theater.
Automated tool results create the *appearance* of compliance.
They don't produce the documented, human-validated record that any of these certifications or audits require.

## Workplace Accommodations

Source: JAN/DOL, *Accommodation and Compliance: Low Cost, High Impact*, 2023.
https://www.dol.gov/newsroom/releases/odep/odep20230504

- Nearly 50% of workplace accommodations cost nothing to implement.
- Median one-time cost for accommodations that do have a cost: $300.
- 68.4% of employers reported accommodations as very or extremely effective.
- More than half made accommodations specifically to retain valued employees.

**Note on applicability:** This data covers workplace accommodations, not digital product accessibility directly.
The "low cost, high impact" framing may be useful if the article addresses the objection that accessibility work is expensive or disruptive.
