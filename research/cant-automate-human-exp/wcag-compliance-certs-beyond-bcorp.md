## Regional Compliance as a Quality Signal: RGAA and the EAA

### The French RGAA: Transparency Through Percentages

France's Référentiel Général d'Amélioration de l'Accessibilité (RGAA) is a robust framework that requires public and certain large private organizations (those with over €250 million turnover) to be exceptionally transparent about their digital accessibility status.
Unlike many other regions where a simple binary "compliant" or "non-compliant" is used, the RGAA mandates the display of a "Compliance Percentage".

Organizations must conduct comprehensive audits against 106 precise tests and publish an Accessibility Statement (Déclaration d'Accessibilité) in a legally formatted manner.

- **Fully Compliant:** A badge indicating 100% compliance.
- **Partially Compliant:** Displays a score between 50% and 99%, along with a public list of non-compliances and a remediation plan.
- **Non-Compliant:** A score below 50%.

This mandatory display serves as a powerful "public naming" mechanism, incentivizing companies to improve their scores to protect their brand image.
Furthermore, the Access-Score project in France provides an independent, open-source rating from A to E, allowing the public to scrutinize the state of digital accessibility across over 28,000 websites.
For an organization, achieving an "A" rating or a "Fully Compliant" status under RGAA is a prestigious mark of technical excellence and ethical integrity.

### The European Accessibility Act (EAA) and Market Differentiation

The European Accessibility Act (EAA), which becomes enforceable on June 28, 2025, is set to harmonize accessibility requirements across the European Union for products and services such as e-commerce, banking, smartphones, and computers.
The EAA is aligned with the four POUR principles and essentially mandates WCAG 2.1 Level AA for all in-scope digital services.

While the EAA is a legislative directive, it provides organizations with a significant opportunity to "stand out" by being early adopters.
Companies that can demonstrate EAA compliance through conformance reports and third-party audits will find it easier to trade across borders within the EU, reducing costs associated with divergent national rules.
